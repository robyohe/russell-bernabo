About / Contact

Russell Bernabo has conserved objects for museums, institutions, and private collections since graduating from the Winterthur Program in Early American Culture in 1989.

His practice centers on gilded frames and surfaces, with additional work in leather, ceramics, plaster, metals, marble, painted surfaces, and wooden artifacts. Frames are part of the big picture — and their conservation demands the same rigor as the works they surround.

Clients include institutions across the museum community and private collectors who understand that careful, ethical treatment and a modest budget are not mutually exclusive.

Civil discourse by phone or email.
